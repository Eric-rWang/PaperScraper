## Topic 1 — Non-Invasive Monitoring of Venous Blood Oxygen (SvO₂ / ScvO₂ / Peripheral Venous Oxygenation)

> **One new-to-repo entry this cycle (date-flagged 2025, not brand-new).** No venous-specific non-invasive monitoring paper published *after* 2026-06-19 met the bar. The entry below is a 2025 paper newly catalogued in this repository. It is **non-optical** (CO₂-kinetics / capnodynamics) and is included because Topic 1 is defined broadly as *non-invasive monitoring of venous blood oxygen* — not optical-only — and because it is a rare cohort-scale, blood-gas-validated non-invasive SvO₂ result.

### Paper 1.1
**Title:** Validation of the capnodynamic method to calculate mixed venous oxygen saturation in postoperative cardiac patients
**Authors:** Mats Wallin, Magnus Hallbäck, Hareem Iftikhar, Elise Keleher, Anders Aneman
**Year:** 2025 (published 7 March 2025)
**Venue:** *Intensive Care Medicine Experimental*, vol. 13 (2025), article 741
**Link / DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1186/s40635-025-00741-z ; PMC11889286 (https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC11889286/)
**Metadata verified:** Title, journal (*Intensive Care Medicine Experimental*), 2025 publication, DOI (10.1186/s40635-025-00741-z), PMC ID (PMC11889286), cohort (**47 haemodynamically stable postoperative cardiac patients**), reference standard (**mixed venous blood gas from a pulmonary-artery catheter**, sampled during stepwise PEEP/alveolar-recruitment changes), and the headline agreement statistics (**bias −0.02% [95% CI −0.96–0.91], limits of agreement +8.8% / −8.9%**) cross-confirmed across the Springer/ICMx listing and the PMC index. **Could NOT confirm on source (flagged):** exact author order/affiliations beyond the cross-indexed list, and per-step subgroup values (Springer/PMC full text returned 403).
**Relevance score:** 7 / 10
*(Genuinely **venous**, genuinely **non-invasive**, and **blood-gas-validated at cohort scale** against PA-catheter SvO₂ — the gold standard the optical thread is chasing. Downgraded from higher because it is (a) **not an optical/imaging method** — it infers mixed-venous SvO₂ from CO₂ kinetics and effective pulmonary blood flow in **mechanically ventilated** patients, so it is not label-free, non-contact, or applicable to spontaneously breathing subjects; and (b) it yields a single global mixed-venous number, not a vessel-resolved or site-specific saturation.)*

**Novelty:** A clinical validation of a **capnodynamic** non-invasive SvO₂ estimate. The capnodynamic method measures **effective pulmonary blood flow from CO₂ elimination kinetics** during controlled ventilation; combined with breath-by-breath CO₂ elimination and the Fick relationship, it back-calculates a non-invasive approximation of **mixed venous oxygen saturation** without a pulmonary-artery catheter. The new contribution is the **head-to-head validation in real patients** (47 postoperative cardiac cases) against simultaneous PA-catheter mixed-venous blood gas, performed across deliberate oxygen-delivery perturbations (stepwise PEEP recruitment). The reported near-zero bias (−0.02%) with ±~9% limits of agreement is a credible, if not yet interchangeable-grade, agreement for a fully non-invasive systemic SvO₂ readout.

**Integration insights:** This paper supplies a **non-optical, non-MRI reference point** for the project's central question — how close can a *non-invasive* SvO₂ estimate get to PA-catheter truth? It sets a concrete bar (~±9% LoA at cohort scale) that any optical venous oximeter aiming at *systemic* SvO₂ must eventually beat or match, and it does so in exactly the ICU/perioperative population where ScvO₂/SvO₂ matters clinically. It also reframes the repository's "MRI is the only cohort-scale calibrator" thesis: MRI (McDiarmid 2026 T₂ iSvO₂; Skow 2025 susceptometry) is the *imaging* calibrator, but **capnodynamics is a competing, ventilator-based, continuous-capable non-invasive SvO₂ method** already in clinical validation — a relevant benchmark and, potentially, a co-validation partner for an optical method (an optical sO₂ at an accessible vein could be cross-checked against capnodynamic mixed-venous SvO₂ in the same ventilated patient). Honest caveats: requires mechanical ventilation and controlled CO₂ kinetics (not ambulatory/wearable), gives a global mixed-venous value (no vessel/site resolution), and is outside the optical-imaging modality family that is the project's main focus — hence catalogued as a Topic-1 *benchmark*, not an optical breakthrough.

### Paper 2.1
**Title:** Retinal oxygen saturation as a non-invasive estimate for mixed venous oxygen saturation and cardiac output
**Authors:** K. Van Keer, J. Van Keer, J. Barbosa Breda, et al. (lead author **Van Keer**; full author list **not verified on source** — Wiley full text returned HTTP 403; *flagged*)
**Year:** 2019
**Venue:** *Acta Ophthalmologica* (Wiley), 2019
**Link / DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1111/aos.13890 ; PMID 30280510 (https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/30280510/) ; Wiley PDF https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/pdf/10.1111/aos.13890
**Metadata verified:** Title, journal (*Acta Ophthalmologica*), year (2019), DOI (10.1111/aos.13890), PMID (30280510), lead author (Van Keer), study design (**dual-wavelength retinal oximetry** of retinal arterial SaO₂-retinal and **venous SvO₂-retinal**, in subjects undergoing **right-heart catheterization** for invasive mixed-venous SvO₂ and cardiac output), and the headline statistics — **age-adjusted SvO₂-retinal vs SvO₂-mixed β = 0.80, p = 0.003**, and **retinal A–V saturation difference vs the inverse of cardiac output ρ = 0.59, p = 0.026** — cross-confirmed across the PubMed record and the Wiley listing. **Could NOT confirm on source (flagged):** full author list and exact n / cohort composition (Wiley/PMC full text 403).
**Relevance score:** 7 / 10
*(The most direct **optical-imaging-to-true-SvO₂** link in the repository: an optical (retinal-oximetry) venous-saturation readout **validated against pulmonary-artery-catheter mixed-venous SvO₂** in the same humans. Downgraded from higher because (a) it is a **small pilot/proof-of-concept** with a moderate age-adjusted correlation, not an interchangeability validation; (b) retinal venous saturation is a **regional CNS-vessel** readout, not the systemic mixed-venous blood it is being correlated to — the correlation is physiological, not identity; and (c) the technique is commercial dual-wavelength fundus oximetry, not a new modality. It is 2019, hence date-flagged — but it was absent from all seven prior scans and answers the project's core validation question more directly than any optical paper catalogued so far.)*

**Novelty:** A **proof-of-concept that non-invasive retinal oximetry can estimate systemic mixed-venous oxygenation and cardiac output.** Where the repository's other retinal-oximetry entries (SP-SFDI, BMC macular oximetry, Gu et al. vis-OCT, the Frontiers carotid-stenosis study) correlate retinal venous saturation to *ocular/CNS disease*, this study correlates it to the **systemic hemodynamic gold standard**: retinal venous saturation tracks PA-catheter mixed-venous SvO₂ (age-adjusted β = 0.80), and the **retinal arteriovenous oxygen-extraction difference** tracks the inverse of cardiac output (ρ = 0.59). It reframes the eye as a non-invasive window not just onto local retinal metabolism but onto **whole-body oxygen delivery/extraction balance**.

**Integration insights:** This is the **missing validation archetype** the project keeps flagging — an optical venous-saturation measurement put head-to-head with invasive systemic SvO₂ in the same subjects — now found, in the retina, in humans. It directly complements today's Topic-1 capnodynamic paper (Paper 1.1): both validate a *non-invasive* SvO₂ estimate against PA-catheter truth, one via CO₂ kinetics and one via optical retinal oximetry, bracketing the optical and non-optical approaches around the same gold standard. It anchors the repository's "retina as proving ground" thesis to a **systemic** endpoint rather than a purely ocular one, and it gives the **A–V difference / oxygen-extraction-fraction** readout — the physiologically meaningful venous endpoint the project repeatedly invokes — a concrete link to cardiac output. The integration lesson for any optical venous oximeter (retinal, jugular-PA, or peripheral): the eventual validation target is PA-catheter SvO₂, the correlation will be physiological rather than identity, and it should be reported age-adjusted, since this study needed age correction to reach significance. Honest caveats: small pilot, moderate correlation, regional-CNS vessel standing in for systemic blood, commercial fundus-oximetry technique (not a new modality), 2019 (date-flagged), and full author list unverified on source.

## Cross-Topic Synthesis

### What this scan actually establishes
On a **one-day cadence** in a low-volume niche, the venous-specific non-invasive oxygen literature **again produced no brand-new publication** — the correct and expected outcome, now observed for the sixth consecutive short-cycle scan. The value added today is **two new-to-repository papers, both validated against the systemic-venous gold standard (PA-catheter SvO₂)** — one non-optical (Topic 1) and one optical (Topic 2) — that together close a specific, long-standing gap in this repository: *no catalogued paper had yet put a non-invasive SvO₂ estimate head-to-head with invasive mixed-venous truth.* Now two have, by independent routes.

1. **A non-optical non-invasive SvO₂ benchmark enters the record (Paper 1.1).** Capnodynamic (CO₂-kinetics) mixed-venous SvO₂, validated in 47 postoperative cardiac patients against PA-catheter blood gas (bias −0.02%, LoA ±~9%). It sets a concrete, cohort-scale accuracy bar for *any* non-invasive SvO₂ method — including optical ones — and identifies a ventilator-based competitor/co-validation partner to the MRI calibrators. A companion trial (NCT06632197) extends it.
2. **The most direct optical-to-true-SvO₂ bridge enters the record (Paper 2.1).** Van Keer et al. 2019 retinal oximetry vs PA-catheter mixed-venous SvO₂ (age-adjusted β = 0.80) and the retinal A–V difference vs 1/cardiac-output. It elevates the "retina as proving ground" thesis from an ocular-disease endpoint to a **systemic-hemodynamic** one, and supplies the validation archetype the optical thread has been missing.
3. **No new modality breakthrough.** Today's photoacoustic/HSI/SFDI/vis-OCT hits all map to already-catalogued work; the optoacoustic cerebral-venous item is carried as a lead to verify.

### Persisting gaps (carried forward, unchanged and still open)
- **No new in-vivo human venous-PA sO₂ result.** The 2023 IJV-PA proof-of-concept (arXiv:2303.10775) remains the only direct human named-vein photoacoustic oximetry result; clinical-scale validation still does not exist.
- **The systemic-validation gap is now partly addressed — but only in the retina and only at pilot scale (optically).** Paper 2.1 is a *small pilot* with a moderate, age-adjusted correlation; Paper 1.1 reaches cohort scale but is *non-optical* and ventilator-bound. A cohort-scale, blood-gas-validated, label-free *optical* SvO₂ at an accessible vein is still unpublished.
- **Vessel-specific venous separation remains proven mainly in the retina (and thin/murine tissue).** Extension of single-vessel venous separation to an accessible systemic vein in thick human tissue is still unshown.
- **Topic 1's forward motion is mostly trials.** Four venous-relevant trials are now tracked (hepatic-vein NIRS, post-arrest jugular SjO₂, MRI-reference ScvO₂, and the new capnodynamic SvO₂ trial); only the capnodynamic method now has a *published* cohort-scale validation.

### Bottom line
A near-**empty cycle for brand-new publications**, used to add **two honest, gold-standard-validated, new-to-repository data points** — a 2025 capnodynamic non-invasive SvO₂ validation (Topic 1, non-optical benchmark) and a 2019 retinal-oximetry-vs-mixed-venous-SvO₂ pilot (Topic 2, the optical-to-true-SvO₂ bridge). Both are date-flagged as not brand-new, and both are scored honestly (7/10) with clear caveats. Nothing this cycle alters the standing picture, but it does sharpen one corner of it: **the validation target for any non-invasive venous oximeter is PA-catheter SvO₂, the agreement bar is ~±9% (capnodynamic) / a moderate age-adjusted correlation (optical retinal), and the systemic, label-free, cohort-scale optical equivalent remains the unfilled prize.** No arterial-only padding was added to reach five entries per topic, per the scan's honesty mandate.
