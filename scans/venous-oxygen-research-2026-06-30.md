## Topic 1 — Non-Invasive Monitoring of Venous Blood Oxygen (SvO₂ / ScvO₂ / Peripheral Venous Oxygenation)

> **Sparseness flag — two not-yet-catalogued entries, no brand-new post-06-29 venous-specific publication.** Both entries below are **non-optical** (capnodynamic CO₂-rebreathing; vital-signs analytics) and are therefore tangential to this repository's *optical-imaging* goal — but both are genuine, human-validated, non-invasive *venous-saturation* methods not previously logged, so they are catalogued with honest, modest relevance scores.

### 1.1 — Validation of the capnodynamic method to calculate mixed venous oxygen saturation in postoperative cardiac patients *(newly catalogued — not previously surfaced; lead entry for the Capno-SvO₂ family)*
- **Authors:** Mats Wallin, Magnus Hallbäck, Hareem Iftikhar, Elise Keleher, Anders Aneman
- **Year / Venue:** **2025 (March 7)** · *Intensive Care Medicine Experimental* **13(1):32**
- **Link / DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1186/s40635-025-00741-z · PMC11889286
- **Relevance:** **6.5 / 10** — a *true non-invasive mixed-venous SvO₂* method validated against the pulmonary-artery-catheter (PAC) blood-gas gold standard in humans. **Docked** because it is **non-optical** (capnodynamic CO₂ rebreathing via the ventilator), so it does not directly serve this repository's *optical-imaging* endpoint — but it is one of the very few non-invasive techniques that delivers the *systemic* SvO₂ number the optical thread is chasing, and so is an important accuracy benchmark.
- **Novelty:** The capnodynamic method derives effective pulmonary blood flow and, via a Fick rearrangement, **mixed venous SvO₂** from CO₂ kinetics during brief inspiratory-hold breathing-pattern changes — requiring **no blood sampling and no catheter**. This study reports the first formal agreement analysis in **47 haemodynamically stable postoperative cardiac patients**, comparing Capno-SvO₂ against mixed venous blood-gas sampled from a PAC. It anchors a fast-maturing 2024–2025 family: Svedmyr et al. *Sci. Rep.* (Nov 2024, DOI `10.1038/s41598-024-77483-7`, porcine endotoxemic/sepsis model) and Svedmyr et al. *Anesth. Analg.* (2024, anesthetized children) extend the same method into sepsis physiology and pediatrics.
- **Integration insights:** This is the **non-optical counterpart to PA-TEE** (catalogued 06-29 §2.1): both target *systemic mixed venous SvO₂*, the PAC endpoint, non-/minimally-invasively. Capno-SvO₂ proves that a *clinically actionable, continuously trackable mixed-venous number* is already obtainable at the bedside without optics — which sharpens the bar the optical program must clear: any optical SvO₂ method (IJV-PA, decoloring-PACT) has to justify itself against a CO₂-rebreathing method that is already in ventilated patients. It also flags the most realistic near-term *fusion* play — an optical venous-sO₂ readout cross-checked against, or calibrated by, a capnodynamic SvO₂ in the same ventilated ICU patient. **Verification:** title/author-list/journal/volume/article-number(32)/DOI/date cross-confirmed via the Springer *ICMx* listing **and** the PMC index (PMC11889286) **and** a ProQuest record; Springer and PMC full text returned 403, so claims beyond the abstract/index are not read on the publisher page and are flagged. The Svedmyr *Sci. Rep.* 2024 and *Anesth. Analg.* 2024 family members were cross-confirmed via Nature and PubMed index entries respectively.

### 1.2 — The Inadequate Delivery of Oxygen (IDO₂) index as a non-invasive surrogate for venous oxygen saturation *(newly catalogued — not previously surfaced)*
- **Authors / Anchor papers:** Adult validation — Anstey et al. (and colleagues), *JTCVS Open* (2024), article `S2666-2736(24)00254-7`. Pediatric correlation — *Pediatric Cardiology* (2023), DOI `10.1007/s00246-023-03302-x` (PMID 37743384); pediatric IDO₂-vs-laboratory-ScvO₂ — *(2025)* PMC11741213.
- **Year / Venue:** **2023–2025** · *JTCVS Open* / *Pediatric Cardiology* / pediatric-ICU analytics literature
- **Link / DOI:** https://www.jtcvsopen.org/article/S2666-2736(24)00254-7/fulltext · https://doi.org/10.1007/s00246-023-03302-x · PMC11741213
- **Relevance:** **4 / 10** — a *non-invasive venous-saturation surrogate*, but an **algorithmic/analytics** one (the T3 `IDO₂` index is computed from routinely monitored vital signs and labs to output the probability that SvO₂/ScvO₂ is below a threshold), not a direct measurement and **not optical**. Catalogued for completeness of the non-invasive-venous landscape and because it is the operational benchmark most ICUs actually use today; scored modestly for this repository's optical goal.
- **Novelty:** The IDO₂ index is a machine-learning analytic embedded in the Etiometry **T3** platform that continuously estimates the probability of *inadequate oxygen delivery* — operationally, the probability that mixed/central venous saturation has fallen below a user-set threshold (commonly SvO₂ < 40 % or ScvO₂ < 50 %). The 2023–2025 validations newly catalogued here close the loop empirically: in the **pediatric cardiac ICU**, IDO₂-50 identified venous saturation < 50 % with AUC ≈ 0.80; an **adult cardiovascular-ICU** validation (*JTCVS Open* 2024) extends the association to adults; and a **2025 pediatric study** correlated the index against *laboratory* ScvO₂ measurements directly.
- **Integration insights:** IDO₂ defines the **incumbent non-invasive venous-monitoring standard of care** that any optical SvO₂ method must displace or augment. It is purely inferential — it never *measures* hemoglobin saturation, it infers risk from hemodynamics — which is exactly the gap an optical venous oximeter (vessel-resolved PA, cerebral-NIRS venous surrogate, capnodynamic SvO₂) would fill with a real saturation value. The most useful framing for synthesis: the field already has a *risk-probability* venous surrogate in wide clinical use (IDO₂) and an emerging *physiological-measurement* venous number (Capno-SvO₂, IJV-PA); the optical program's value proposition is a **continuous, vessel-specific, true-saturation** readout that beats the inferential surrogate on specificity. **Verification:** the *JTCVS Open* 2024 adult-validation and *Pediatric Cardiology* 2023 articles and the 2025 pediatric ScvO₂-correlation (PMC11741213) were cross-confirmed via PubMed/journal index listings and ResearchGate; all full-text fetches returned 403, so author lists and numeric results beyond the indexed abstracts are flagged as not read on a publisher page.

### 2.1 — A flexible optoacoustic blood "stethoscope" for noninvasive multiparametric cardiovascular monitoring *(newly catalogued — not previously surfaced)*
- **Authors:** Haoran Jin, Zesheng Zheng, Zequn Cui, Ying Jiang, Geng Chen, Wenlong Li, Zhimin Wang, Jilei Wang, Chuanshi Yang, Weitao Song, Xiaodong Chen, Yuanjin Zheng (Nanyang Technological University)
- **Year / Venue:** **2023 (Aug)** · *Nature Communications* **14**, art. 4805
- **Link / DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1038/s41467-023-40181-5 · PMC10403590 · PMID 37542045
- **Relevance:** **7 / 10** for *venous specificity* — a **non-invasive, wearable, flexible optoacoustic patch** that images blood **oxygen saturation in both arteries *and* veins**, explicitly **discriminates hypoxia trends from veins** (something pulse oximetry cannot do, since SpO₂ reads only the arterial pulsatile component), and was demonstrated **on human subjects** for venous elasticity and arterial flow-mediated dilation. **Docked** because the *venous sO₂ imaging* was shown principally in mice (the human work centred on venous elasticity/FMD, not a validated human venous-sO₂ number) and it is a 2023 device, not a post-06-29 publication.
- **Novelty:** A conformable optoacoustic sensor combining light-delivery elements with piezoelectric acoustic receivers in a skin-mounted patch, giving **continuous multiparametric cardiovascular readouts** — oxygen saturation (optoacoustic amplitude correlated to sO₂ with R² ≈ 0.99), blood-vessel elasticity, pulse-wave dynamics, and exogenous-contrast metabolism — from a single wearable. Its decisive venous feature: because optoacoustic contrast is generated directly from hemoglobin absorption in the targeted vessel (not from arterial pulsatility), the device can read **venous oxygenation directly**, separating arterial and venous hypoxia trends.
- **Integration insights:** This is the **wearable-form-factor venous-optoacoustic entry the repository lacked.** Where IJV-PA (arXiv:2303.10775) is a benchtop dual-wavelength PA/US system reading one named deep vein and PA-TEE is an esophageal probe, the blood "stethoscope" demonstrates the *form factor* — a flexible skin patch — most likely to make continuous non-invasive venous oximetry deployable, and it directly validates the central physical premise of the whole optical-venous program: **optoacoustic contrast reads venous sO₂ directly, free of the arterial-pulsatility constraint that limits pulse oximetry to SpO₂.** Its gap defines the next milestone exactly: translate the *quantitative, calibrated venous-sO₂ number* demonstrated in mice to validated humans — which is precisely the job of the catalogued quantitative-PA decoloring thread (ISDC 06-25 §2.1, convex-cone 06-24 §2.1, qPACT 06-11 §2.4, Hybrid-Net 06-10 §2.4). **Verification:** title/full-author-list/journal/volume(14)/article(4805)/DOI/PMC/PMID and the "images sO₂ in arteries and veins / discriminates venous hypoxia / human venous elasticity" claims cross-confirmed via the Nature article listing, the PMC index (PMC10403590), PubMed (37542045), and the Springer Nature "Research Communities" author post; all full-text fetches returned 403, so numeric results beyond the indexed abstract are flagged as not read on a publisher page.

### 2.2 — Significant Correlation Between Retinal Blood Flow and Oxygen Saturation During Intravitreal Aflibercept Treatment for Central Retinal Vein Occlusion *(newly catalogued — not previously surfaced)*
- **Authors:** Yukiko Miyoshi, Yuki Nakano, Yuta Koyama, Rie Osaka, Junichiro Akimitsu, Aki Booka, Kiyoshi Suzuma
- **Year / Venue:** **2025 (June 30)** · *Translational Vision Science & Technology (TVST)* **14(6):35** (ARVO)
- **Link / DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1167/tvst.14.6.35 · PMC12212447
- **Relevance:** **6 / 10** for *venous specificity* — a **vessel-resolved retinal venous oximetry** clinical study reporting an explicit, dynamic **retinal venular sO₂** value (34.1 % → 41.1 %). **Docked** because it is **retina-only** and uses **fundus-camera dual-wavelength reflectometry (Oxymap T1)** — a tissue-surface reflectance method, not a depth-resolved or systemically-transferable one — so it informs vessel-resolved venous oximetry methodology more than it advances a deep-vein measurement.
- **Novelty:** In 32 untreated non-ischemic CRVO eyes, combined **Oxymap T1 retinal oximetry + laser speckle flowgraphy (LSFG)** tracked retinal hemodynamics through monthly intravitreal aflibercept. The venous finding is the salient one: **retinal venous sO₂ was depressed at baseline (34.1 %)** — consistent with venous stasis and increased oxygen extraction in occlusion — and **rose significantly to 41.1 % at one month** after anti-VEGF, correlating with restored venous blood flow and visual-acuity recovery. It demonstrates that retinal *venous* saturation is a treatment-responsive, longitudinally trackable optical biomarker.
- **Integration insights:** Reinforces the repository's standing thesis that **the retina is the proving ground for vessel-specific optical venous oximetry** — here, retinal *venular* sO₂ behaves as a quantitative, intervention-responsive readout of venous oxygen extraction, exactly the vessel-resolved venous signal the deep-tissue methods aspire to. It pairs naturally with the catalogued vis-OCT (parafoveal baseline PMC9133487) and HSI (PMC12997856) retinal-venous work and the DR-staging clinical thread (06-24 §2.2), extending the retinal evidence base from *cross-sectional disease staging* to *longitudinal treatment response*. The caution carried from 06-19 (retinal venous sO₂ can reflect systemic/CNS hypoxia, not just local disease) applies. **Verification:** title/full-author-list/journal/volume(14)/issue(6)/article(35)/DOI/date and the 34.1 %→41.1 % venous-sO₂ figures cross-confirmed via the TVST/ARVO listing, the PMC index (PMC12212447), and an independent search index; ARVO and PMC full text returned 403, so figures beyond the indexed abstract are flagged as not read on a publisher page.

- **Authors:** Zuzana Schreiberová, Samuel Genzor, Tomáš Mudroch, Barbora Šindelářová, Petra Hübnerová, Miroslava Malušková, Klára Marešová, Martin Šín, Marta Karhanová

## Cross-Topic Synthesis

Today is the **thirteenth scan**, one day after the twelfth, in a niche that genuinely publishes venous-specific work at very low volume. As expected, **no brand-new (post-2026-06-29) venous-specific primary paper appeared**. The cycle's value is **five not-yet-catalogued items** — three of them 2025 — that **add a new axis** to the standing picture rather than merely refreshing it:

1. **Two human-validated *non-optical* venous methods now anchor the field's accuracy bar.** Capno-SvO₂ (Topic 1, §1.1) computes *systemic mixed venous SvO₂* non-invasively from CO₂ kinetics and is now validated against the PAC blood-gas gold standard in postoperative cardiac patients (2025), with sepsis-model and pediatric extensions. The IDO₂ index (Topic 1, §1.2) is the *incumbent* non-invasive venous surrogate already deployed in adult and pediatric ICUs, validated against laboratory ScvO₂. Together they reframe the optical program's value proposition: the clinic already has both a *physiological-measurement* venous number (Capno-SvO₂) and a *risk-probability* surrogate (IDO₂) without optics — so an optical venous oximeter must justify itself on **vessel specificity, continuity, and true per-vessel saturation**, the three things neither incumbent provides.

2. **The optoacoustic "stethoscope" (Topic 2, §2.1) is the wearable-form-factor venous-optoacoustic entry the repository was missing.** It validates the program's core physical premise — *optoacoustic contrast reads venous sO₂ directly, free of the arterial-pulsatility limit that confines pulse oximetry to SpO₂* — and demonstrates it in a flexible skin patch on humans. Its gap (quantitative human venous-sO₂ still mouse-stage) is exactly the job of the catalogued quantitative-PA decoloring thread, restating the standing throughline: **the physics works; calibrated, depth-resolved human venous sO₂ is the remaining translational step.**

3. **The retina keeps proving the vessel-resolved venous concept (Topic 2, §2.2–2.3).** The 2025 CRVO study shows retinal *venular* sO₂ is a treatment-responsive biomarker (34→41 % under anti-VEGF); the 2025 post-COVID cohort shows it is reproducible at cohort scale but *locally* bounded (null systemic signature 1.5 years out). Both extend the retinal evidence base — from cross-sectional staging to longitudinal response and normative stability — while re-flagging the standing caution that retinal venous sO₂ is a *local ocular* signal absent independent systemic anchoring.

4. **The persisting gaps are unchanged and still open.** (a) **No *non-invasive* human venous-PA SvO₂ at clinical scale** — the 2023 IJV-PA proof-of-concept (sijvO₂ 72 ± 7%) remains the only direct *non-invasive* human named-vein PA result; PA-TEE is minimally invasive/porcine; the optoacoustic stethoscope's human venous-sO₂ number is not yet validated. (b) **The MRI / now-also-Capno-SvO₂ validation-scale gap dominates** — both MRI venous-SvO₂ and Capno-SvO₂ are blood-gas-validated at cohort scale; no *optical* method approaches that bar.
