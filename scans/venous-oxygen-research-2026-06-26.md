**Strongest standing venous-monitoring references (all *previously surfaced* — NOT new):**

1. **Hill, Campbell, Chase & Pretty (2024) — Estimation of Venous Oxygen Saturation Through Non-Invasive Optical Sensing at the Jugular Veins.** *Current Directions in Biomedical Engineering.* DOI `10.1515/cdbme-2024-2072` (catalogued 06-10 §1.1). **Relevance 9/10.** The repository's closest thing to a true non-invasive *named-vein* optical SvO₂ result — EJV SvO₂ ≈ 70–72% (pulse) / 74–75% (breathing) across 3 subjects, in striking agreement with the IJV-PA result below. *Previously surfaced; re-confirmed today via De Gruyter Brill + ResearchGate listing; no new version.*
2. **Sun et al. (2024) — IJV NIRS + Monte-Carlo + DNN.** *Opt. Lett.* 49:2669 (06-10 §1.2). **Relevance 8/10.** Model-based deep-vein NIRS SvO₂ retrieval. *Previously surfaced.*
3. **Jeleff et al. (2023) — Mespere VO100 jugular NIRS, 79-patient critically-ill cohort.** PMID 37827917 (06-10 §1.3). **Relevance 7.5/10.** Largest clinical evaluation of a commercial non-invasive jugular-venous oximeter on file. *Previously surfaced.*
4. **Oh et al. (2023) — Noninvasive tracking of mixed venous saturation via cerebral-NIRS oximetry.** DOI `10.1038/s41598-023-49078-1` (06-10 §1.4). **Relevance 7/10.** Retrospective demonstration that rScO₂ tracks SvO₂ trends (rScO₂ is ~70–75% venous-weighted). *Previously surfaced; re-confirmed today.*
5. **Zhang et al. (2016/2017) — induced-venous-fluctuation peripheral SvO₂.** DOIs `10.1186/s12938-016-0208-8` and `10.1186/s12938-017-0351-x` (06-17 §1.3). **Relevance 7/10.** The "artificial venous pulse" principle — exploiting arterio-venous compliance differences to make venous blood pulsatile for a pulse-oximeter-style readout. *Previously surfaced; re-confirmed today.*

**Other catalogued venous-monitoring references (NOT re-entered):** McDiarmid et al. 2026 cardiac-MRI T₂ SvO₂ (06-11 §1.1); Skow et al. 2025 MRI susceptometry venous oximetry (06-14 §1.1); Lahade et al. 2026 wearable FD-NIRS arterio-venous transition (06-11 §1.2); Tan et al. 2023 arterial+venous optical e-tattoo (06-17 §1.1); hDOS 2025 Durduran-group venous-occlusion platform (06-17 §1.2); Mileder et al. 2022 preterm-neonate NIRS venous-occlusion, 226 infants, DOI `10.3389/fped.2022.834045` (06-24 §1.1); peripheral-venous-vs-co-oximetry validation + artificial-venous-pulse PPG family (06-22).

## Topic 2 — Optical Imaging Techniques for Non-Invasive Venous Blood Oxygen

> **Sparseness flag — NO new entry this cycle.** No genuinely new primary optical-imaging study reporting *venous* saturation, and no not-yet-catalogued transferable imaging paper, surfaced after 06-25. The strongest standing references are restated below for self-containment; none is a new finding.

**Strongest standing optical-imaging references (all *previously surfaced* — NOT new):**

1. **Noninvasive in vivo photoacoustic measurement of internal jugular venous oxygenation in humans** (arXiv 2303.10775; catalogued in prior scans). **Relevance 9.5/10.** Dual-wavelength PAT+US imaged the IJV in **7 healthy volunteers**, yielding sijvO₂ = **72 ± 7%** — the most direct human deep-vein *optical* SvO₂ result on file, and the cleading proof-of-concept for the whole program. *Previously surfaced; re-confirmed today via arXiv + ADS; no new version.*
2. **Wu et al. (2025) — convex-cone multispectral-PAT sO₂.** IEEE *Trans. Med. Imaging* (06-24 §2.1). **Relevance 8/10.** Model-based (non-learning) spectral-coloring correction for vascular sO₂. *Previously surfaced.*
3. **Wang et al. (2026) — Integrated Spectral and Depth Compensation (ISDC) for sO₂/THb in transvaginal US-guided PAT.** *J. Biomed. Opt.* 31(2):026002, DOI `10.1117/1.JBO.31.2.026002` (06-25 §2.1). **Relevance 7/10.** Pairs spectral *and* depth fluence correction, validated on deep clinical human PAT data — directly transferable to deep-vein PA sO₂. *Previously surfaced.*
4. **Adaptive spectroscopic vis-OCT (ADS-vis-OCT) retinal oximetry** (Communications Medicine `10.1038/s43856-023-00288-8`; vis-OCT family catalogued in prior scans). **Relevance 8/10.** Vessel-resolved retinal sO₂ with per-vessel spectral-contaminant removal; ex-vivo bias ~1% (0–100%), in-vivo vein-vs-artery repeatability SD 2.3%/2.5%. The retina is the one bed where *vessel-specific* (hence venous-specific) optical oximetry is routine — the template the deep-vein problem aims to reach. *Previously surfaced.*
5. **Photoacoustic vector tomography for deep haemodynamic imaging** (PMC11136879; catalogued 06-10/06-21). **Relevance 7.5/10.** Haemodynamic quantification of deep veins beyond 5× the optical diffusion limit (>5 mm) — the depth regime where veins live. *Previously surfaced.*

**Other catalogued optical-imaging references (NOT re-entered):** SP-SFDI / BioxyDR retinal αSO₂ methods (Basiri et al., 06-14 §2.1) and the Sept-2025 *Eye* DR-staging clinical paper (`10.1038/s41433-025-04032-5`, 06-24 §2.2); the Oct-2025 awake-mouse cerebral-PA oxygenation bioRxiv preprint (06-24 §2.3); the vascular-graph-network OR-PAM ovarian-lesion paper (*Photoacoustics* `10.1016/j.pacs.2025.100794`, 06-25 §2.2); Cam et al. qPACT virtual-imaging framework (06-11 §2.4); Hybrid-Net 3-D vascular sO₂ (06-10 §2.4); the PINN optical-inversion/spectral-unmixing arXiv 2602.16357 (06-11); the deep-learning quantitative-spectroscopic-PA arXiv 2512.15394 (06-19→06-25); the sulfate-phantom NIR-PA arXiv 2512.01458 (06-14 onward); the JIOHS PACT-oximetry review (06-17 §2.1); the PACT spatiotemporal-flow / oxygenation-heterogeneity human-vein paper (PMC11161372, 06-22/06-24); HSI retinal/tissue oximetry and the Aug-2025 comparative NIRS/SFDI/PPG/TcPO₂ paper (`10.1038/s41598-025-15767-2`); the Frontiers carotid-stenosis retinal-oximetry caution (06-19 §2.1); the snapshot-SFDI adversarial-deep-learning tissue-oxygenation paper (PMC7701163).

2. **The unfilled prize is unchanged.** A **continuous, vessel-resolved, non-invasive optical SvO₂ at a *named deep vein*, validated at clinical scale** remains demonstrated only in small proof-of-concept cohorts: the IJV-PA work (sijvO₂ = 72 ± 7%, n = 7) and the jugular optical-sensing EJV result (Hill 2024, ≈ 70–72%, n = 3). Their close agreement (~72%) across two independent optical modalities is the strongest cross-validation the field has, and it has not been extended this cycle.

3. **The two structural levers are also unchanged.** Progress continues to flow through (a) the **fluence / spectral-coloring problem** (ISDC, convex-cone TMI, qPACT, Hybrid-Net, the PINN and deep-learning-spectroscopic preprints) — the depth-and-wavelength correction that gates deep-vein PA sO₂; and (b) **vessel-level identification** (vascular-graph OR-PAM, vis-OCT per-vessel segmentation) — the step that turns a per-voxel sO₂ map into a *venous* number. Stitching the two (build a vascular graph → label vessels arterial vs venous → apply a depth/spectral-corrected per-vessel sO₂ estimator) remains the concrete near-term route to deep-tissue venous-specific optical oximetry, the capability the retina already enjoys but deep beds do not.
