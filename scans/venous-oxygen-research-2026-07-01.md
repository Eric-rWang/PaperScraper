### 1.1 — Wearable Optical E-Tattoo for Arterial and Venous Blood Oxygenation Monitoring *(newly catalogued patent artifact — the underlying peer-reviewed paper is already logged; no new patient data)*
- **Inventors:** Philip (P.) Tan, E. Wang, S. Tamma, S. Bhattacharya, Nanshu Lu — University of Texas at Austin (govt. support: Army Research Office grant W911NF-19-2-0333)
- **Year / Venue:** **US Patent Application, published/issued 2025-01-23** · Application **No. 20250025074** (USPTO)
- **Link:** https://patents.justia.com/patent/20250025074 · Google Patents: https://patents.google.com/patent/US20250025074A1/en
- **Relevance:** **4 / 10** — genuinely *venous-specific* (the device is explicitly designed for **simultaneous arterial *and* venous** blood-oxygenation monitoring, with the venous signal confirmed to arise from wrist **venous pulsatility**), but this is a **patent document, not a new study**: it contains no new validation cohort beyond the already-catalogued Tan et al. 2023 paper, and it is **non-invasive optical PPG at a peripheral (wrist) site**, i.e. peripheral venous oxygenation rather than central/mixed SvO₂. Scored modestly for exactly these reasons.
- **Novelty:** The application claims an ultra-thin, self-adherable **optical e-tattoo** laminated directly over a co-located artery and vein (wrist), using a multi-location PPG array and a signal-separation algorithm to disentangle the arterial from the venous pulse and output **both** arterial and venous SO₂ from the same patch. The venous readout is derived from **venous pulsatility** rather than an induced venous occlusion — a distinguishing feature from the venous-occlusion-test family (Zachia Alan 2022, Mileder 2022, hDOS 2025). The IP formalizes the method demonstrated in the catalogued conference paper and stakes claims on the arterio-venous separation algorithm.
- **Integration insights:** This is the **provenance/IP anchor** for the repository's only *wearable, simultaneous arterial-and-venous, truly non-invasive optical* venous-oximetry thread (Tan et al. 2023, §1.1 of scan 06-17). Its value for synthesis is chiefly (a) confirming that the UT-Austin group is pursuing **commercial translation** of peripheral venous PPG oximetry, and (b) reinforcing that **venous pulsatility** (not just venous occlusion) is a viable non-invasive venous signal source — a complementary approach to the induced-fluctuation methods (Zhang 2016/2017) and the jugular-pulse optical sensor (Hill 2024). It does **not** advance the central/mixed-SvO₂ goal (the clinically decisive endpoint), which remains the province of jugular PA/NIRS, CMR, and capnodynamic methods. **Verification:** title / application number (20250025074) / publication date (2025-01-23) / inventor list (Tan, Wang, Tamma, Bhattacharya, Lu) / UT-Austin assignee / ARO grant number cross-confirmed via the Justia patent record **and** an independent search index; Justia and Google Patents full pages both returned HTTP 403 to direct fetch, so claim text beyond the indexed abstract is not read on the source page and is flagged. The underlying peer-reviewed paper (Tan et al., *Towards Simultaneous Noninvasive Arterial and Venous Oxygenation Monitoring with Wearable E-Tattoo*, PMID 38083768) is **already catalogued** (scan 06-17 §1.1) and is **not** re-entered.
