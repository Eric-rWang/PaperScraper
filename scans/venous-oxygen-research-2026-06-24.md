### 1.1 — Non-invasively Measured Venous Oxygen Saturation as Early Marker of Impaired Oxygen Delivery in Preterm Neonates *(newly catalogued — not previously surfaced)*
- **Authors:** Lukas P. Mileder, Julia Buchmayer, Nariae Baik-Schneditz, Bernhard Schwaberger, Nina Höller, Chad C. Andersen, Michael J. Stark, Gerhard Pichler, Berndt Urlesberger
- **Year / Venue:** 2022 · *Frontiers in Pediatrics*, vol. 10, art. 834045
- **Link / DOI:** https://doi.org/10.3389/fped.2022.834045 · PMC8831784 · https://www.frontiersin.org/journals/pediatrics/articles/10.3389/fped.2022.834045/full
- **Relevance:** **6.5 / 10** — true *peripheral venous* saturation, measured non-invasively in a large human cohort, but older work and a single-tissue-bed surrogate rather than systemic SvO₂/ScvO₂.
- **Novelty:** Applies non-invasive NIRS with the **venous-occlusion test** to derive **peripheral (forearm/calf muscle) SvO₂** and fractional oxygen extraction in **226 preterm neonates** (median GA 33.9 weeks), and asks whether early venous-saturation/extraction changes flag inflammation/infection in the first 72 h. A genuinely venous-endpoint study at cohort scale.
- **Integration insights:** This is the clinical embodiment of the **venous-occlusion-test thesis** that recurs across this repository (Zachia Alan 2022 thenar StO₂→ScvO₂; the Durduran-group hDOS platform). It demonstrates that NIRS + induced venous stasis yields a *venous-specific* number with prognostic signal in a real population — exactly the surrogate pathway the optical-imaging topic is trying to make vessel-resolved and continuous. Useful as a clinical-validation anchor for the peripheral-venous branch, and a reminder that the venous-occlusion surrogate already has cohort-scale human evidence where direct optical deep-vein sO₂ does not. **Verification:** title/authors/journal/DOI cross-confirmed via Frontiers listing + PMC index; full text 403.

### 2.1 — Blood Oxygenation Quantification in Multispectral Photoacoustic Tomography Using a Convex Cone Approach *(newly catalogued — not previously surfaced)*
- **Authors:** Chuhua Wu, Hongzhi Zuo, Manxiu Cui, Handi Deng, Yuwen Chen, Xuanhao Wang, Bangyan Wang, Cheng Ma (Tsinghua University, THU-BPLab)
- **Year / Venue:** **July 2025** · *IEEE Transactions on Medical Imaging*, vol. 44, no. 7
- **Link / DOI:** https://ieeexplore.ieee.org/document/10929704 (IEEE Xplore doc 10929704; exact 10.1109/TMI DOI suffix **not directly verifiable** in this environment — see caveat) · code: https://github.com/thu-bplab/Convex-Cone-Approach
- **Relevance:** **7 / 10** — not venous-specific (general/arterial blood sO₂), but a rigorously-validated quantitative-PA method **directly transferable** to venous sO₂, *with human in-vivo experiments*.
- **Novelty:** Derives, from a rigorous light-transport model, that PA spectra for distinct sO₂ values are confined within **separate convex cones (CCs)**; sO₂ is then estimated by finding the nearest cone to the measured spectrum via a modified **Gilbert–Johnson–Keerthi (GJK)** algorithm. This sidesteps the central obstacle of quantitative PA oximetry — the unknown, spatially-varying fluence/scattering that corrupts the measured spectrum — using a single spatial location's data, without per-pixel fluence inversion or large training sets. Validated across simulation, phantoms, **and human in-vivo experiments**.
- **Integration insights:** Spectral-coloring (depth-dependent fluence distortion) is precisely *why* deep-vein PA sO₂ is hard, and veins sit deeper than the superficial arterioles most PA oximetry validates against. A model-based, training-free correction that holds at a single location and works in human tissue is exactly the kind of estimator needed to push PA oximetry from microvascular sO₂ toward a named deep vein. Slots directly alongside the catalogued quantitative-PACT thread (Cam et al. qPACT virtual-imaging framework 06-11 §2.4; the JIOHS PACT-oximetry review 06-17 §2.1; Hybrid-Net 06-10 §2.4) as a **non-learning** alternative to the data-driven decoloring methods. **Verification:** title/author-list/venue/volume cross-confirmed via Semantic-Scholar/ablesci index + the authors' GitHub repo (which contains a `Human_experiment` folder); IEEE full text and the Crossref/S2 DOI API returned 403, so the exact DOI string is flagged unverified.

### 2.2 — Assessing and monitoring abnormal retinal blood circulation for early detection of pre-structural damage and enhanced diabetic-retinopathy staging using non-invasive, high-resolution biophotonic imaging technology *(newly catalogued — distinct from the previously-surfaced SP-SFDI methods paper)*
- **Authors:** (BioxyDR™ / SP-SFDI group; author list not fully resolvable from indexes in this environment — flagged)
- **Year / Venue:** **September 2025** · *Eye* (Nature / RCOphth), vol. 39, issue 16
- **Link / DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1038/s41433-025-04032-5 · https://www.nature.com/articles/s41433-025-04032-5
- **Relevance:** **7.5 / 10** — reports a vessel-resolved retinal **venous** oxygenation analogue (αSO₂) in a human clinical-staging context; docked half a point because αSO₂ is an SFDI-derived *analogue* of true sO₂ and the underlying method was previously surfaced.
- **Novelty:** A clinical-translation paper for the **Saccadic-Phase Spatial-Frequency-Domain Imaging (SP-SFDI)** platform, commercialized as **BioxyDR™**. Using two snapshots that capture phase shifts in spatially-modulated light, it measures an oxygen-saturation analogue (αSO₂) in the superficial retinal vasculature with **<3% oxygenation sensitivity**, and shows **retinal venous αSO₂ differs significantly between controls and diabetic-retinopathy patients**, enabling objective DR staging and *pre-structural* detection. Positions SFDI retinal oximetry as a deployable clinical biomarker, not just a methods demo.
- **Integration insights:** Extends the already-catalogued **SP-SFDI methods work (Basiri et al., scan 06-14 §2.1)** into a clinical-outcomes publication, reinforcing the repository's central retinal-venous thesis: the retina remains the one accessible bed where *vessel-specific* optical venous saturation is being validated against disease endpoints. It also adds SFDI to the short list of modalities (alongside vis-OCT and HSI) producing a venous-specific retinal number, and — read against the Frontiers carotid-stenosis caution (06-19 §2.1) — adds a *positive* disease-discrimination result for retinal venous oxygenation where that earlier study found confounding. **Verification:** title/journal/volume/issue/DOI cross-confirmed via Nature listing + ResearchGate index; full text 403, author list and exact cohort size not resolvable and flagged.

- **Authors:** (not resolvable from index in this environment — flagged)

## Cross-Topic Synthesis

### What this scan actually establishes
On a two-day cadence in a low-volume niche, **no brand-new (post-2026-06-22) venous-specific paper appeared** — the expected outcome, now seen across every short-cycle scan in this series. The cycle's value is in **clearing four genuinely-not-yet-catalogued items off the backlog** rather than re-confirming an empty set:
1. **A model-based quantitative-PA estimator with human data (convex-cone, IEEE *TMI* 2025).** Attacks spectral coloring — the exact obstacle between microvascular and deep-vein PA oximetry — without learning or per-pixel fluence inversion. The strongest transferable-technique addition this cycle.
2. **A clinical retinal-venous SFDI result (BioxyDR, *Eye* 2025).** Promotes the already-catalogued SP-SFDI method into a disease-discrimination publication, adding a *positive* venous-oxygenation biomarker result and reinforcing the retina-as-proving-ground thesis.
3. **An awake, intact-skull cerebral-PA oximetry preprint (bioRxiv 2025).** Tissue-level/murine, but removes anesthesia and craniotomy confounders — a methodological stepping-stone for in-vivo venous-PA.
4. **A cohort-scale clinical venous-occlusion NIRS study (preterm neonates, *Front. Pediatr.* 2022).** The clinical embodiment of the venous-occlusion surrogate that recurs throughout this repository; cohort-scale human evidence the optical-imaging side still lacks for direct deep-vein sO₂.

### Persisting gaps (carried forward, unchanged and still open)
- **No new in-vivo human venous-PA sO₂ result.** The 2023 IJV-PA proof-of-concept (sijvO₂ 72 ± 7%) is still the only direct human named-vein photoacoustic oximetry on record. The convex-cone method is a tool that *could* help close this, not a venous result.
- **The MRI validation-scale gap still dominates.** Two MRI venous-SvO₂ methods are blood-gas-validated at cohort scale; no optical method approaches that bar. An MRI-reference ScvO₂ validation trial (NCT05161884) is registered.
- **Vessel-specific venous separation remains proven mainly in the retina (and thin/murine tissue).** Today's new entries (SFDI retina; awake-mouse cerebral PA) stay within that boundary; extension to an accessible systemic vein in thick human tissue is still unshown.
- **The venous-occlusion surrogate is the one venous endpoint with cohort-scale human validation** (preterm-neonate NIRS, §1.1; thenar StO₂→ScvO₂; hDOS) — yet it is single-tissue-bed and intermittent, not vessel-resolved or continuous.
- **Confounding remains a documented obstacle** (Frontiers carotid-stenosis study); the BioxyDR DR-staging result (§2.2) is a counter-data-point where retinal venous oxygenation *did* discriminate disease.

### Bottom line
No brand-new publication this cycle, reported honestly. The cycle's contribution is **catching up the backlog**: one quantitative-PA method with human data (convex-cone), one clinical retinal-venous SFDI result (BioxyDR), one transferable awake-mouse cerebral-PA preprint, and one cohort-scale clinical venous-occlusion NIRS study (preterm neonates). Nothing alters the standing picture — **MRI is the absolute-reference calibrator, the retina is the proving ground, the venous-occlusion test is the practical (and best human-validated) surrogate, and human deep-vein optical sO₂ at clinical scale remains the unfilled prize.** No arterial-only padding was added to reach five entries per topic.
