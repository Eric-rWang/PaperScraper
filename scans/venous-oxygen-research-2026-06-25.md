### 2.1 — Integrated Spectral and Depth Compensation Approach for Optimizing Oxygen Saturation and Total Hemoglobin Estimation in Photoacoustic Tomography for Ovarian Lesion Diagnosis *(newly catalogued — not previously surfaced)*
- **Authors:** Lukai Wang, Yixiao Lin, Haolin Nie, Jinhua Xu, Sanskar Thakur, Quing Zhu (Washington University in St. Louis)
- **Year / Venue:** **4 February 2026** · *Journal of Biomedical Optics* (SPIE), vol. 31, no. 2, art. 026002
- **Link / DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1117/1.JBO.31.2.026002 · PMC12869027 · https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC12869027/
- **Relevance:** **7 / 10** — not venous-specific (general blood %sO₂ + THb in a mixed arterial/venous bed), but a rigorously-validated quantitative-PA fluence-correction method **directly transferable** to deep-vein sO₂, with clinical human PAT data at depth.
- **Novelty:** Tackles the central obstacle of quantitative PA oximetry — wavelength- **and** depth-dependent optical-fluence variation that corrupts the measured spectrum — by combining a **Monte-Carlo-derived spectral compensation** (correcting spectral coloring) with an explicit **depth-wise fluence correction** into one integrated pipeline (**ISDC**). Validated on phantoms with known optical properties and applied to **clinical transvaginal US-guided PAT data from 82 ovarian lesions (67 benign, 15 malignant)**, improving the accuracy of %sO₂ and total-hemoglobin biomarkers for malignant-vs-benign discrimination.
- **Integration insights:** Spectral coloring and depth-dependent fluence falloff are *exactly* why deep-vein PA sO₂ is hard — veins sit deeper than the superficial arterioles most PA oximetry validates against, so the error this paper targets is the error that has kept human deep-vein optical sO₂ out of reach. An integrated correction validated on **deep, clinical, in-vivo human PAT data** (transvaginal geometry reaching centimeter-scale depth) is a strong methodological transfer candidate for the IJV / deep-peripheral-vein PA problem. It slots directly alongside the catalogued quantitative-PACT thread — Cam et al. qPACT virtual-imaging framework (06-11 §2.4), the convex-cone TMI paper (06-24 §2.1), the JIOHS PACT-oximetry review (06-17 §2.1), and Hybrid-Net (06-10 §2.4) — as another **model-based (non-learning) fluence-decoloring** approach, this one uniquely pairing spectral *and* depth correction and proving it on a large clinical cohort at depth. **Verification:** title/authors/venue/volume/issue/article/DOI cross-confirmed via SPIE Digital Library DOI string + PMC index + ResearchGate listing; PMC and Europe PMC full text returned 403, so cohort details beyond the 82-lesion figure are taken from the abstract/index and not read on a publisher page.

### 2.2 — Vascular Graph Network for Ovarian Lesion Classification Using Optical-Resolution Photoacoustic Microscopy *(newly catalogued — not previously surfaced)*
- **Authors:** Yixiao Lin, Lukai Wang, Ian S. Hagemann, Lindsay M. Kuroki, Brooke E. Sanders, Andrea R. Hagemann, Cary Siegel, Matthew A. Powell, Quing Zhu (Washington University in St. Louis)
- **Year / Venue:** **30 December 2025** · *Photoacoustics* (Elsevier), in press / 2025 volume
- **Link / DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1016/j.pacs.2025.100794 · PMC12813362 · https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S221359792500117X
- **Relevance:** **4.5 / 10** — adjacent, not venous. This is a **vessel-morphology classification** paper (graph-neural-network on OR-PAM microvascular maps), not an oximetry/sO₂ paper, and its target is ovarian-lesion malignancy, not venous oxygenation. Included honestly as a genuinely-new, not-yet-catalogued Topic-2 item with a modest score, not as padding.
- **Novelty:** Represents OR-PAM-imaged microvasculature as a **graph** (vessels → edges/nodes with geometric and topological attributes) and trains a **graph network** to classify benign vs malignant ovarian lesions from vascular architecture — moving PA-microscopy analysis from pixel/CNN features to explicit vessel-network topology.
- **Integration insights:** Only indirectly relevant to venous sO₂, but it matters as a methods signpost: **vessel-level graph representations** are the natural substrate for *labeling individual vessels as arterial vs venous* before assigning a saturation — the segmentation/identification half of the deep-vein problem that quantitative methods (2.1, the convex-cone paper, Hybrid-Net) leave open. A pipeline that first builds a vascular graph and *then* applies a per-vessel sO₂ estimator is a plausible route to vessel-specific (and thus venous-specific) optical oximetry. Catalogued for that architectural connection and to keep the WashU OR-PAM/PAT program fully tracked; not a venous-oxygen result in itself. **Verification:** title/full-author-list/venue/DOI cross-confirmed via ScienceDirect article ID + PMC index + ResearchGate; full text 403, exact volume/article-number and page range not resolvable on the publisher page and flagged.

## Cross-Topic Synthesis

Today is an honest low-yield cycle — eleventh scan, one day after the tenth — and it sharpens, rather than changes, the standing picture:

1. **Venous *monitoring* (Topic 1) produced nothing new today.** No not-yet-catalogued paper surfaced, confirming that the practical venous-monitoring literature (jugular NIRS, Mespere VO100, the venous-occlusion test, the artificial-venous-pulse PPG family, MRI references) is now essentially fully captured in this repository. The unfilled prize is unchanged: a **continuous, vessel-resolved, non-invasive optical SvO₂ at a *named deep vein*, validated at clinical scale** — something still demonstrated only in small proof-of-concept cohorts (Hill 2024 EJV; the IJV-PA work).

2. **Both of today's new items are quantitative-PA *enabling methods*, not venous results.** That is the recurring shape of the genuinely-new literature: the field is advancing on the **fluence/spectral-coloring problem** (2.1's ISDC, the convex-cone TMI paper, qPACT, Hybrid-Net, the PINN preprint) far faster than on venous-specific endpoints. The ISDC paper is the strongest of today's pair because it pairs **spectral *and* depth** correction and proves it on **deep, clinical, in-vivo human PAT data** — precisely the depth regime where veins live and where prior PA oximetry has been weakest. If deep-vein PA sO₂ is ever to work in humans, it will be on the back of corrections like this.

3. **The two halves of the deep-vein problem are visibly converging in one lab.** The WashU group is simultaneously publishing (a) *better per-voxel sO₂ quantification at depth* (2.1) and (b) *vessel-network representation* (2.2). Stitching these — build a vascular graph, label vessels arterial vs venous, then apply a depth/spectral-corrected sO₂ estimator per vessel — is a concrete, near-term route to **vessel-specific (hence venous-specific) optical oximetry** in deep tissue, the exact capability the retina already enjoys (SP-SFDI, vis-OCT, HSI) but deep beds do not.

4. **No arterial-only padding was added.** Honest count for 2026-06-25: **0 new Topic 1 entries, 2 new (transferable, non-venous) Topic 2 entries.** The synthesis carries the full standing reference set so it remains self-contained without re-reading prior files.
