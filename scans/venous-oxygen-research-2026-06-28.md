### 1.1 — Capnodynamic non-invasive mixed venous oxygen saturation (SvO₂) — method + 2025 PAC validation + active trial *(newly catalogued — not previously surfaced)*
- **Anchor publications (the thread, freshest first):**
  - **Validation 2025:** *Validation of the capnodynamic method to calculate mixed venous oxygen saturation in postoperative cardiac patients* — **2025**, *Intensive Care Medicine Experimental* (Springer), DOI `10.1186/s40635-025-00741-z` · PMC12869027-adjacent index PMC11889286. Agreement study vs pulmonary-artery-catheter mixed-venous blood-gas SvO₂ in **47 haemodynamically-stable postoperative cardiac patients**, measurements taken during stepwise PEEP/alveolar-recruitment changes.
  - **Original method:** *Non-invasive capnodynamic mixed venous oxygen saturation during major changes in oxygen delivery* — Hällsjö Sander / Sander et al., **2021/2022**, *Journal of Clinical Monitoring and Computing* (Springer), DOI `10.1007/s10877-021-00762-5` · PMID 34609659 · PMC8490846.
  - **Preclinical:** *Capnodynamic assessment of mixed venous oxygen saturation in a porcine experimental endotoxemic model* — **2024**, *Scientific Reports*, DOI `10.1038/s41598-024-77483-7`.
  - **Active trial:** **NCT06632197** — *Non-invasive Monitoring of Mixed Venous Oxygen Saturation Using the Capnodynamic Method in Adults* (ClinicalTrials.gov), comparing capnodynamic SvO₂ accuracy/correlation against the pulmonary-artery-catheter reference.
- **Year / Venue:** 2021 → 2025 (peer-reviewed) + 2024+ (active trial). Freshest peer-reviewed anchor: **2025**, *Intensive Care Med. Exp.*
- **Link / DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1186/s40635-025-00741-z · https://doi.org/10.1007/s10877-021-00762-5 · https://doi.org/10.1038/s41598-024-77483-7 · https://clinicaltrials.gov/study/NCT06632197
- **Relevance:** **6.5 / 10** — a genuinely *non-invasive, continuous, true mixed-venous SvO₂* estimate validated against the PAC gold standard, which is exactly the Topic-1 endpoint. Marked down two points because it is **non-optical** (it is a CO₂-kinetics / capnography method requiring a closed ventilator circuit), so it is **not transferable to the optical-imaging arm** and only applies to **mechanically-ventilated** patients — but it is the single most clinically-mature non-invasive *whole-body* SvO₂ technique not previously in this repository.
- **Novelty:** Derives **mixed venous** SvO₂ entirely from the respiratory side. The capnodynamic equation estimates effective pulmonary blood flow (cardiac output) from cyclic CO₂-elimination changes induced by a controlled ventilatory pattern; combined with breath-by-breath VCO₂ and the Fick principle, it back-calculates SvO₂ without any blood sampling or intravascular catheter. The 2025 paper is the first agreement study against a simultaneous PAC blood-gas reference in real postoperative cardiac patients across deliberately perturbed oxygen-delivery states (PEEP steps) — i.e. it tests the method where SvO₂ is *moving*, not just at a single steady point.
- **Integration insights:** This is the **non-optical counterpart** to the project's central goal, and it is valuable to the repository precisely as a *contrast and a reference target*, not as a competing imaging method. (i) **It re-anchors what "good" looks like for a non-invasive SvO₂ number** — any optical venous oximeter (NIRS, PA, the artificial-venous-pulse PPG family) ultimately wants the same mixed-venous endpoint the capnodynamic method already reports against PAC; the 2025 agreement statistics become a benchmark bar. (ii) **It is mechanistically orthogonal** (gas exchange vs photon absorption), so it could *co-validate* or *fuse* with an optical method in ventilated ICU patients — a capnodynamic global SvO₂ plus a jugular-NIRS / IJV-PA regional SvO₂ would cross-check whole-body vs named-vein saturation, the kind of multi-modal validation the repository repeatedly flags as missing (cf. the MRI absolute-reference thread, McDiarmid 2026 / Skow 2025). (iii) Its **ventilator-only** limitation sharpens *why the optical route still matters*: capnodynamic SvO₂ is unavailable in spontaneously-breathing, ambulatory, neonatal-on-CPAP, or wearable settings — exactly the populations the optical venous-oximetry program targets. **Verification:** trial ID, all four DOIs/PMIDs and the 47-patient cohort figure cross-confirmed across PubMed, Springer index, ClinicalTrials.gov and ResearchGate listings; publisher full text returned 403, so agreement statistics (bias/limits-of-agreement) beyond the cohort size and design were not read on a publisher page and are flagged as unverified-at-source.

### 2.1 — Score-Based Diffusion Models for Diffuse Optical Tomography with Uncertainty Quantification *(newly catalogued — not previously surfaced)*
- **Authors:** Fabian Schneider, Meghdoot Mozumder, Konstantin Tamarov, Leila Taghizadeh, Tanja Tarvainen, Tapio Helin, Duc-Lam Duong
- **Year / Venue:** **3 February 2026** · arXiv preprint **2602.03449** [stat.ML / cs.LG / stat.AP]
- **Link / DOI:** https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.03449 · https://arxiv.org/pdf/2602.03449
- **Relevance:** **4.5 / 10** — methods-only and not venous-specific (and not even oxygenation-specific): it reconstructs the **absorption + scattering** maps that *precede* any sO₂ estimate, and demonstrates on linearised difference-imaging, not on a vascular/venous target. Included honestly as the one genuinely-new, not-yet-catalogued Topic-2 item, scored modestly rather than as padding. Transferable to the venous problem only at the inverse-problem layer.
- **Novelty:** Brings **score-based diffusion (generative) priors** with the **UCoS** (unconditional representation for the conditional score function) framework to the DOT inverse problem, and — crucially for a quantitative venous endpoint — delivers **calibrated Bayesian uncertainty quantification** on the reconstructed optical-property maps. DOT is severely ill-posed (diffuse near-infrared boundary measurements → interior absorption/scattering), so a learned data-driven prior that also yields posterior uncertainty is a meaningful step beyond deterministic / Tikhonov-regularised reconstructions.
- **Integration insights:** Venous (and tissue) oxygenation in diffuse-optics is computed from reconstructed **oxy- and deoxy-haemoglobin absorption**; the accuracy and the *error bars* of an SvO₂/StO₂ number are therefore inherited directly from the quality of this upstream absorption reconstruction. A diffusion-prior reconstruction that returns **per-voxel uncertainty** is exactly what a clinically-credible non-invasive venous oximeter needs — it lets a downstream sO₂ estimate carry a confidence interval rather than a bare point value, the same gap the repository's qPACT / Hybrid-Net / PINN spectral-unmixing thread keeps circling. It slots alongside the catalogued data-driven-inversion line (Cam et al. qPACT virtual-imaging framework, 06-11 §2.4; the "Deep Learning-Driven Quantitative Spectroscopic PA" arXiv 2512.15394; the PINN optical-inversion preprint arXiv 2602.16357, 06-11) as another **learned-prior, uncertainty-aware inverse-problem** approach — here on the *diffuse-optical* (deep-tissue, non-imaging-resolution) side rather than the photoacoustic side. Honest caveats: no haemoglobin, no oxygenation, no vessel and no venous target appear in the paper; it is a reconstruction-methodology contribution whose relevance is strictly upstream-and-transferable. **Verification:** title, full author list, arXiv ID, 3 Feb 2026 date and subject classes confirmed directly on the fetchable arXiv abstract page and cross-checked against the arXiv listing; no journal version found, flagged as preprint-only.

## Cross-Topic Synthesis

Today is an honest low-yield cycle — twelfth scan, three days after the eleventh — and the two genuinely-new items, read together, sharpen rather than change the standing picture:

1. **The one new Topic-1 item is non-optical, and that is the point.** The **capnodynamic mixed-venous SvO₂** method is the most clinically-mature non-invasive *whole-body* SvO₂ technique not previously in this repository, validated against the pulmonary-artery catheter (2025 *Intensive Care Med. Exp.*, 47 patients) and now in an active accuracy trial (NCT06632197). It matters to the optical program as a **benchmark and a complement, not a competitor**: it defines the agreement bar an optical venous oximeter must clear, it is mechanistically orthogonal (gas exchange vs photon absorption) so it could *co-validate or fuse* with jugular-NIRS / IJV-PA in ventilated ICU patients, and — being ventilator-only — it maps out exactly the spontaneously-breathing, ambulatory, neonatal and wearable populations where **only the optical route can go.** The unfilled prize is unchanged: a continuous, vessel-resolved, non-invasive *optical* SvO₂ at a *named deep vein*, validated at clinical scale.

2. **The one new Topic-2 item is an inverse-problem enabling method, again — and again with uncertainty as the headline.** The score-based-diffusion **DOT** preprint (arXiv 2602.03449) is the diffuse-optical sibling of the photoacoustic qPACT/Hybrid-Net/PINN thread: a **learned prior + calibrated posterior uncertainty** for the ill-posed reconstruction that *precedes* any oxygenation estimate. The recurring shape of the genuinely-new literature holds — the field advances on the **reconstruction / fluence / spectral-coloring / uncertainty** machinery faster than on venous-specific endpoints. A credible clinical venous oximeter needs sO₂ numbers that carry error bars; today's preprint supplies that machinery on the deep-tissue diffuse-optical side, where veins live and resolution is worst.

3. **Two duplicate false-alarms were caught and excluded.** The *JACC: Advances* "Noninvasive Model of Mixed Venous Oxygen Saturation in Heart Failure" is the already-catalogued **McDiarmid 2026** cardiac-MRI T₂ paper (same DOI/authors), and the PA "thrombosis-induced deep-tissue oxygenation" paper is the already-catalogued **Feng et al.** (PMC12047737). Surfacing both prominently, then verifying them against the back-catalogue and *not* re-entering them, is the deduplication mandate working as intended.
