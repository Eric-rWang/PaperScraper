### 1.1 — Near-infrared spectroscopy-based cerebral oxygen monitoring in perioperative neurocognitive disorders of older adults: current evidence and future perspectives *(newly catalogued — not previously surfaced)*
- **Authors:** Yuhan Meng, Ruyi Xia, Meiru Jiang, Tao Li, Qiaoxia Sun, Jiahai Ma
- **Year / Venue:** **2026 (March)** · *Frontiers in Aging Neuroscience*, vol. 18, art. 1809264
- **Link / DOI:** https://doi.org/10.3389/fnagi.2026.1809264 · PMC13102829 · https://www.frontiersin.org/journals/aging-neuroscience/articles/10.3389/fnagi.2026.1809264/full
- **Relevance:** **4 / 10** — a 2026 review of NIRS cerebral-oxygen (rSO₂) monitoring, which is a *mixed arterio-venous tissue* measurement weighted ~70–75% venous, **not** vessel-resolved SvO₂. Relevant to the repository's cerebral-NIRS venous-surrogate thread and current, but a review and tissue-level, so scored modestly.
- **Novelty:** Synthesizes current evidence and future directions for **NIRS-based cerebral rSO₂ monitoring** as a perioperative-management tool to predict and mitigate **perioperative neurocognitive disorders (PND)** in older surgical patients, organizing risk across pre-/intra-/post-operative stages (age and comorbidity; intraoperative cerebral hypoperfusion and high-risk surgery; postoperative neuroinflammation and sleep disturbance) and arguing for multi-stage, NIRS-guided intervention. A 2026 currency update to the clinical case for cerebral oximetry rather than a new measurement method.
- **Integration insights:** The cerebral rSO₂ that this review centres on is the *same signal* the repository's catalogued respiration-frequency-filtered "spiroximetry" work (PMC4126245 / PMID 24439329) and the Oh et al. 2023 retrospective (DOI `10.1038/s41598-023-49078-1`) exploit to extract a **cerebral venous** saturation surrogate — the venous compartment dominates the NIRS-interrogated volume, which is why respiration-gated or trend-based methods can recover an SvO₂-like number. This review is useful as a current (2026) clinical-rationale anchor for why a *venous-resolved* refinement of cerebral NIRS matters, and as a reminder that the large installed base of perioperative NIRS oximeters is the most plausible near-term deployment surface for any venous-specific algorithm layered on top. Not a venous measurement in itself. **Verification:** title/full-author-list/journal/volume/article-number/DOI cross-confirmed via the Frontiers listing **and** the PMC index (PMC13102829); Frontiers full text returned 403, so claims beyond the abstract/index are not read on the publisher page and are flagged.

### 2.1 — In-vivo Continuous Monitoring of Mixed Venous Oxygen Saturation by Photoacoustic Transesophageal Echocardiography (PA-TEE) *(newly catalogued — not previously surfaced)*
- **Authors:** Lihong V. Wang group (Caltech/WashU); first author resolvable from the ADS bibcode suffix as **Li (Lei Li et al.)** — **full author list not directly verifiable in this environment and flagged.**
- **Year / Venue:** **2016** · *Proc. SPIE* **9689**, "Photons Plus Ultrasound: Imaging and Sensing 2016" / BiOS conference "Diagnostic and Therapeutic Applications of Light in Cardiology" (paper 9689-101; Conference Presentation)
- **Link / DOI:** ADS bibcode `2016SPIE.9689E..2UL` · https://ui.adsabs.harvard.edu/abs/2016SPIE.9689E..2UL/abstract · ResearchGate publication 301711533 · SPIE conference page https://spie.org/PW16B/conferencedetails/cardiology (exact `10.1117/12.*` DOI suffix **not resolvable** in this environment — flagged)
- **Relevance:** **7.5 / 10** for *venous specificity* — this targets true **mixed venous SvO₂** (the pulmonary-artery-catheter endpoint), the single most clinically important venous number, measured by photoacoustic oximetry. **Docked from higher** because it is **minimally invasive** (esophageal probe, ⌀ < 15 mm), a **2016 conference presentation** (not a peer-reviewed full paper), and demonstrated **in swine**, not humans. It is included precisely because it is the repository's only direct photoacoustic SvO₂-vs-gold-standard result, with a clear caveat that it is *not* non-invasive.
- **Novelty:** Integrates **transesophageal echocardiography (TEE)** with **photoacoustic oximetry** in a single dual-modality esophageal probe — a rotatable acoustic array, a flexible optical fibre bundle, and a light-integrating acoustic lens — driven by a mobile OPO laser and a modified ultrasound console, computing and updating **SvO₂ on-line every second**. In living swine, modulating inspired-oxygen fraction produced corresponding changes in PA-TEE-measured SvO₂, demonstrating real-time tracking. It directly attacks the clinical problem that SvO₂ is otherwise obtained only by an invasive pulmonary-artery catheter (PAC) carrying ~10% severe-complication risk.
- **Integration insights:** This is the *endpoint-anchor* the rest of the repository circles around. Where the catalogued IJV-PA proof-of-concept (arXiv:2303.10775) measures a *named peripheral vein* non-invasively and the venous-occlusion / cerebral-NIRS work yields *tissue-bed* venous surrogates, PA-TEE is the only entry that targets **systemic mixed venous SvO₂** — the gold-standard hemodynamic variable — by photoacoustics, validated against the PAC reference modality in vivo. It establishes that **PA oximetry can read a clinically actionable venous number continuously**, and frames the central translational gap sharply: the physics works (PA-TEE, swine), but reaching it *non-invasively* and at depth in humans still depends on exactly the fluence/spectral-coloring corrections the catalogued quantitative-PA thread (ISDC 06-25 §2.1, convex-cone 06-24 §2.1, qPACT 06-11 §2.4, Hybrid-Net 06-10 §2.4) is built to provide. A natural framing for synthesis: PA-TEE is *what success looks like* for the venous endpoint; the decoloring methods are *how* to get there without an esophageal probe. **Verification:** title/year/venue/SPIE-volume(9689)/SvO₂-in-swine claims cross-confirmed via the ADS index entry **and** the ResearchGate publication record **and** the SPIE conference listing; ADS abstract page and SPIE returned 403/blocked to direct fetch, so the **full author list and exact 10.1117 DOI suffix could not be read on a publisher page and are explicitly flagged unverified.**

## Cross-Topic Synthesis

Today is the **twelfth scan**, four days after the eleventh, in a niche that genuinely publishes venous-specific work at very low volume. As expected, **no brand-new (post-2026-06-25) venous-specific primary paper appeared**. The cycle's value is two not-yet-catalogued items that sharpen — rather than change — the standing picture, and together they bracket the field's two ends:

1. **PA-TEE (Topic 2, §2.1) is the endpoint-anchor the repository was missing.** It is the only catalogued result that measures **systemic mixed venous SvO₂** — the pulmonary-artery-catheter gold standard — by photoacoustic oximetry, validated in vivo (swine) against inspired-oxygen modulation. It proves the *physics* of reading a clinically actionable venous number continuously by PA works. Its caveats define the remaining gap precisely: it is **minimally invasive** (esophageal probe), **2016**, **conference-stage**, and **porcine**. Everything else in Topic 2 — the IJV-PA non-invasive named-vein result, and the quantitative-PA decoloring methods (ISDC, convex-cone, qPACT, Hybrid-Net) — is, in effect, the program to reach PA-TEE's number *without the probe and at depth in humans.* Framing the decoloring literature as "how to get to the PA-TEE endpoint non-invasively" is the most useful synthesis this entry enables.

2. **The 2026 Frontiers NIRS review (Topic 1, §1.1) reaffirms the deployment surface for the venous-surrogate branch.** Cerebral rSO₂ is a venous-dominated tissue signal, and the large perioperative installed base of NIRS oximeters is the most plausible near-term home for any venous-resolved algorithm (respiration-gated spiroximetry; cerebral-NIRS ScvO₂ surrogates). It is a clinical-rationale update, not a method — but it keeps the venous-surrogate branch current at 2026.

3. **The persisting gaps are unchanged and still open.** (a) **No *non-invasive* human venous-PA SvO₂ at clinical scale** — PA-TEE is minimally invasive and porcine; the 2023 IJV-PA proof-of-concept (sijvO₂ 72 ± 7%) remains the only direct *non-invasive* human named-vein PA result. (b) **The MRI validation-scale gap still dominates** — two MRI venous-SvO₂ methods are blood-gas-validated at cohort scale; no optical method approaches that bar (NCT05161884 registered). (c) **Vessel-specific venous separation remains proven mainly in the retina** (vis-OCT, HSI, SP-SFDI) and in thin/murine tissue; extension to an accessible *systemic* vein in thick human tissue is still unshown. (d) **The venous-occlusion surrogate** (preterm-neonate NIRS, thenar StO₂→ScvO₂, hDOS) is the one venous endpoint with cohort-scale human validation, yet it is single-tissue-bed and intermittent, not vessel-resolved or continuous.

4. **No arterial-only padding.** Honest count for 2026-06-29: **one new Topic 1 entry (2026 NIRS review, tissue-level) and one new Topic 2 entry (PA-TEE, venous-specific but minimally-invasive, 2016).** The synthesis carries the full standing reference set so it remains self-contained without re-reading prior files.

**Bottom line:** No brand-new publication this cycle, reported honestly. The contribution is two backlog items that bracket the field — PA-TEE establishing what a successful photoacoustic *venous* SvO₂ measurement looks like (and exactly why it is not yet non-invasive in humans), and a 2026 NIRS review keeping the cerebral-venous-surrogate branch current. The standing picture holds: **MRI is the absolute-reference calibrator, the retina is the proving ground, the venous-occlusion test is the best human-validated surrogate, PA-TEE is the endpoint-anchor, and truly non-invasive human deep-vein optical SvO₂ at clinical scale remains the unfilled prize.**
