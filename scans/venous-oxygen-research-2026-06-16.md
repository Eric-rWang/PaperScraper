## Topic 2 — Optical Imaging Techniques for Non-Invasive Venous Blood Oxygen

### Paper 2.1
**Title:** Evaluation of Macular Retinal Oximetry Across Different Levels of Diabetic Retinopathy: A Cross-Sectional Study
**Authors:** Author list not fully verified on source (BMC Ophthalmology 2025 author group; *flagged* — see note)
**Year:** 2025 (published January 2025)
**Venue:** *BMC Ophthalmology* (Springer Nature), vol. 25, article 51
**Link / DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1186/s12886-025-03850-1 (PMC12... open access; PMC11740494)
**Metadata verified:** Title, journal (*BMC Ophthalmology*), year, DOI, PMC ID (PMC11740494), study design, cohort (70 subjects: 28 controls, 26 DM-no-retinopathy, 16 DR [8 mild/moderate NPDR, 8 severe NPDR/PDR]), and the macular retinal-oximetry + OCTA method cross-confirmed across the BMC/Springer listing, the PMC listing, and a Physician's Weekly summary. BMC/Springer/PMC pages all returned 403 — **full author list and exact venular SO₂ numeric values not directly read on source (flagged).**
**Relevance score:** 6 / 10
*(Vessel-resolved retinal oximetry reporting a venular saturation readout; tissue/vasculature SO₂ rather than blood-gas-calibrated venous sO₂, and the headline finding is in the macular superficial capillary plexus rather than a named systemic vein — partly surrogate, but venous-attributed and clinically grounded.)*

**Novelty:** A clinical cross-sectional study quantifying macular retinal oxygen saturation — including a **venular** component — and superficial-capillary-plexus (SCP) vessel density across the full diabetic-retinopathy severity spectrum (control → DM-no-DR → mild/moderate → severe/proliferative). Measurements were taken in a 300–400 µm region at four locations adjacent to the foveal avascular zone, co-registered with OCTA vessel density corrected for refractive error. The work pushes vessel-resolved oximetry from large peripapillary vessels down toward the **capillary level within the macula**, and correlates oxygenation with microvascular density in the same eyes.

**Integration insights:** This is the one genuinely new venous-attributed optical-oximetry paper this cycle, and it reinforces the recurring theme that the retina is the human bed where vessel-specific venous saturation is routinely measured non-invasively. It is methodologically a sibling of the SP-SFDI DR-staging paper (prior scan **2026-06-14 §2.1**, Basiri et al., *Eye*) and the vis-OCT retinal-oximetry literature (prior scans 2026-06-11 §2.5 and 2026-06-14 §2.4): three convergent optical routes — fundus-camera spectral oximetry (this paper), spatial-frequency-domain phase imaging (SP-SFDI), and visible-light OCT spectroscopy — now report retinal venous/venular saturation as a disease biomarker. Its specific contribution is **macular/capillary-level** venular oxygenation tied to vessel density, extending the readout from the large retinal veins toward the microvasculature. The open translational gap is identical to the rest of the retinal literature: capillary-level venular separation demonstrated in the thin, transparent retina must be adapted to thick, opaque peripheral beds before it informs systemic venous monitoring. Honest caveat: the dominant new result is in the SCP, so this is venular-tissue oximetry, not a named-vein blood-gas-calibrated sO₂.

### Paper 2.2
**Title:** Machine Learning–Enabled Multiple Illumination Quantitative Optoacoustic Oximetry Imaging in Humans
**Authors:** Thomas Kirchner, Michael Jaeger, Martin Frenz
**Year:** 2022 (published April 5, 2022)
**Venue:** *Biomedical Optics Express* (Optica), vol. 13, no. 5, pp. 2655–2667 — also arXiv:2202.01482
**Link / DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1364/BOE.456141 ; preprint https://arxiv.org/abs/2202.01482 (PMC9203099, PMID 35774340)
**Metadata verified:** Title, full 3-author list (Kirchner, Jaeger, Frenz), journal, volume/issue/pages (13(5):2655–2667), publication date, arXiv ID, PMC ID, PMID, and the **validation on radial arteries *and accompanying veins* of five healthy human volunteers** cross-confirmed across the BOE/Optica listing, PubMed, PMC, and arXiv index entries. Pages returned 403 — exact DOI suffix (456141) taken from the canonical BOE volume/issue path and **flagged as not directly read on the publisher page**.
**Relevance score:** 6 / 10
⚠️ *Older paper (2022), included because it is **not present in any prior scan in this repository** and is one of the few human optoacoustic oximetry studies that explicitly images and validates against **veins** (radial accompanying veins), not arteries only. Date-flagged: this is best-available context, NOT new work this cycle.*

**Novelty:** Combines **multiple-illumination (MI)** optoacoustic imaging with **multispectral (MS)** acquisition and **learned spectral decoloring (LSD)** — gradient-boosting models that correct for unknown, wavelength-dependent local fluence — to estimate absolute blood sO₂ in real time. Crucially, it validates in vivo on the radial **artery and its accompanying veins** in five humans, i.e., it reports vessel-resolved sO₂ for a paired arterial/venous bed under co-registered ultrasound, addressing the fluence (spectral-coloring) problem that otherwise corrupts deep optoacoustic sO₂.

**Integration insights:** This paper fills a real gap in this project's bibliography: a **human, vessel-resolved, venous-inclusive optoacoustic oximetry** reference that predates and complements the IJV photoacoustic proof-of-concept (prior scan 2026-06-10 §2.1, Garcia-Uribe/Wang). Its LSD spectral-decoloring approach is the conceptual ancestor of the deep-learning unmixing/fluence-correction methods entered in later scans (Hybrid-Net 2026-06-10 §2.4; SPOI-AE 2026-06-11 §2.3; Feng spectral-coloring correction 2026-06-11 §2.2) — and of the newer "distribution-informed, wavelength-flexible data-driven photoacoustic oximetry" line (arXiv:2403.14863, 2024; see honorable mentions). Because it validates on accompanying veins, it is directly on-target for the project's goal and is a useful baseline against which the venous-range sulfate phantoms (2026-06-14 §2.3, Davenet et al.) and physics-informed inversions should be benchmarked. The honest limitation is that the radial accompanying veins are superficial and small; scaling MI-MS-LSD to deeper named veins (IJV, femoral) remains the unproven step.

## Cross-Topic Synthesis

### What this scan actually establishes
On a two-day cadence after a thorough sweep, the **venous-specific non-invasive oxygen literature did not move** — which is itself the honest signal worth recording: this is a genuinely low-volume field, and daily scanning will frequently (correctly) return nothing new for Topic 1. The two Topic 2 additions are bibliographic completeness rather than breaking developments:

1. **A new clinical retinal venular-oximetry data point.** The 2025 BMC Ophthalmology macular-oximetry study (Paper 2.1) extends retinal venous-attributed saturation from large vessels to the macular capillary plexus and ties it to OCTA vessel density across DR severity. It joins SP-SFDI (06-14) and vis-OCT (06-11, 06-14) as the **third** optical modality reporting retinal venous saturation as a disease biomarker — strengthening, but not changing, the "retina as proving ground" thesis.
2. **A previously-missing human venous optoacoustic baseline.** The 2022 Kirchner/Jaeger/Frenz MI-MS-LSD paper (Paper 2.2) is the project's first catalogued human optoacoustic oximetry study validated against *accompanying veins*, and it is the conceptual root of the deep-learning spectral-decoloring/fluence-correction thread the prior scans tracked. Adding it makes the project's PA-oximetry lineage complete from 2016 (eMSOT) → 2021–2022 (LSD / MI-LSD, human veins) → 2023 (human IJV PA) → 2025–2026 (Hybrid-Net, SPOI-AE, spectral-coloring correction, venous-range phantoms).

### Persisting gaps (carried forward, unchanged and still open)
- **No new in-vivo human venous-PA sO₂ result.** The 2023 IJV-PA proof-of-concept remains the only direct human named-vein photoacoustic oximetry result; clinical-scale validation does not exist.
- **The MRI validation-scale gap still dominates.** Two MRI venous-SvO₂ methods (T₂ iSvO₂, susceptometry) are blood-gas-validated at cohort scale; no optical method approaches that bar.
- **Retinal-to-peripheral transfer remains unproven.** All three optical venous-oximetry modalities (fundus oximetry, SP-SFDI, vis-OCT) are retinal; no 2025–2026 feasibility study extends vessel-specific venular separation to an accessible systemic vein.
- **Venous-range calibration is ready before the imaging that needs it.** The sulfate phantoms (06-14 §2.3) and the MI-LSD/eMSOT decoloring methods (now catalogued) exist; the field should publish like-for-like venous-band (≈60–75% sO₂) accuracy numbers across methods rather than reporting on heterogeneous targets.

### Bottom line
An honestly empty Topic 1 and a deliberately small, completeness-oriented Topic 2. The single genuinely new item (BMC retinal macular oximetry, 2025) and the single newly-catalogued human venous optoacoustic baseline (Kirchner et al., 2022) both reinforce — without altering — the standing picture: **MRI is the absolute-reference calibrator, the retina is the proving ground, and human deep-vein optical sO₂ at clinical scale remains the unfilled prize.** No padding was added to reach five entries per topic, per the scan's honesty mandate.
